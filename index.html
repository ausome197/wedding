Events

09th Feb

Mehndi11AM onwards

It's one of the oldest traditional Indian pre wedding ceremony where the bride and groom attend the event together and a professional mehndi artist applies mehndi on the bride's hands and feet. This will be having a celebratory festival feel to it with people wearing vibrant clothes and dancing, singing, playing games and a lot more.

Sangeet Night 6PM - 12AM

Show your dance move, enjoy the music GO BEZERK !!! Together lets kick off the wedding event with a blast.

10th Feb

Tilak & Haldi12PM-2PM

The Tilak ceremony is performed in most of the family according to their personal family tradition and culture. Content to be discussed.

Wedding 8PM onwards

This is the main event in the entire wedding when the bride and the groom tie the knot. The groom comes with the barat and is greeted by the bride side with some dhol of course as maximum number of guests would be attending this event. This is when the couple circle the sacred fire and are finally declared husband and wife.

  Dress code

Ugh... dress codes?

Mehndi11 AM onwards

Ethnic indian wear
Ladies can wear something bright & vivid shades of green and gentlemen can be on their vibrant kurtas with pants or pyjamas along with an optional shawl.

Sangeet7PM - 12AM

Western attire
A semi-formal look that is contemporary & fashionable which is not too prim & proper is the best to pull off this night. You can see some pins for ladies & gents.

Wedding 7PM-12AM

Indo-western
Ladies can flaunt their unconventional sarees, lehengas, anarkalis, dresses and the list goes on whereas gentlemen can wear any kind of suit or just blazer. If you don't like to wear a suit/blazer, you can do a jodhpuri, high-neck (band gala) waistcoat & shirt combo.
